We interact with our computers thousands of times a day. For most of us, this involves a constant, repetitive dance: type some words, grab the mouse, move the cursor to a button, click, return hands to the keyboard, and repeat.

It seems harmless, but what happens when you cut out the mouse entirely?

### Reclaiming Focus

Every time you move your hand to the mouse, your focus shifts slightly. It's a micro-interruption. Over an eight-hour workday, these micro-interruptions add up to a significant amount of mental fatigue.

Ditching the mouse forces you to internalize your environment. You stop clicking randomly and start navigating with intent.
